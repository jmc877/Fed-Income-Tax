Required Texts and Materials

  • Geier, U.S. Federal Income Taxation of Individuals (FIT). The book is free! Some students may find useful to print it out and have it spiral bound.
  • You'll need access to the Code & Regs. I like CCH, Selected Sections, but you can use the Code & Regs published by West or Foundation. I recommend that you keep it next to you to mark up during class and while doing the problems and readings. Given the absurd price of the most recent editions, I highly recommend purchasing (or borrowing) a prior year's edition, e.g., 2023-2024 or even 2022-2023. Don't worry, it will be fine. There will be many changes in 2025, so any version you buy will be out of date in a couple of months. Here's a pdf of the IRC ('19-'20)
  • The Code & Regs are available on line at Tax Materials. This is a fantastic free database of fundamental tax materials. On the Supplemental Materials page, I show how to make a bookmark that will take you directly to the appropriate code or regulation section. All relevant tax materials are also available on BBLaw, WL, Lexis, or Vital Law. Here is a link to the Full IRC as of November, 2024, in all of its full glory, such as amendment history, statutory notes, and effective dates. Finally, you can make your own nicely formatted Code & Regs for free here: Lawsky Code & Regs
  • For each topic, first read FIT and then any additional readings and problems. Use the slides to focus on the important materials that we'll cover during the lectures. Unless otherwise specified, you should do all of the problems in FIT.
