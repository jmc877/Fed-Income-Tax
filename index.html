Required Texts and Materials

  • Geier, U.S. Federal Income Taxation of Individuals (FIT). The book is free! Some students may find useful to print it out and have it spiral bound.
  • You'll need access to the Code & Regs. I like CCH, Selected Sections, but you can use the Code & Regs published by West or Foundation. I recommend that you keep it next to you to mark up during class and while doing the problems and readings. Given the absurd price of the most recent editions, I highly recommend purchasing (or borrowing) a prior year's edition, e.g., 2023-2024 or even 2022-2023. Don't worry, it will be fine. There will be many changes in 2025, so any version you buy will be out of date in a couple of months. Here's a pdf of the IRC ('19-'20).
  • The Code and Regs are available on line at Tax Materials. This is a fantastic free database of fundamental tax materials. On the Supplemental Materials page, I show how to make a bookmark that will take you directly to the appropriate code or regulation section. All relevant tax materials are also available on BBLaw, WL, Lexis, or Vital Law.
  • For each topic, first read FIT and then any additional readings and problems. Use the slides to focus on the important materials that we'll cover during the lectures.

Financial Accounting and Maintenance of Capital Accounts

  • LOGIC: Ch. 4 Slides
  • LOGIC PROBLEMS: Ch. 4, Problems 1 and 2.
    • P1 is an important and challenging problem, and you'll need to have the regulations in front of you when doing it. The problem focuses on Reg. 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv)(a)-(i). There are two approaches: (1) complete a balance sheet (book, tax, OB) for each transaction; or (2) complete a table with each transaction in a separate row and columns corresponding to the effect on the Ps' OB and book capital accounts. It is also helpful to list the appropriate subdivision(s) of the regs that apply in a separate column. For example, $600 of OI increases both A's and B's tax capital accounts (and outside basis) under subdivision (iv)(b)(3). Some hints: For B3, assume that the PSH used its cash to purchase a capital asset and later sold the asset for a loss; for B6, see (iv)(c); for B9 and B10, see (iv)(b) and (e), 731(a)(1), 732(a)(1), 733, and 752.
